Clinical Disease Progression Stages

Understanding the classifications predicted by our Deep Learning model.

Non-Demented

No noticeable cognitive decline. The brain structures are intact with normal volume in regions like the hippocampus, showing standard age-related changes only.

Very Mild Demented

Corresponds to Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI). Small memory lapses, difficulty remembering names or finding objects, while independent daily activities remain normal.

Mild Demented

Early-stage Alzheimer's. Cognitive losses are clear. Individuals find it harder to plan or organize, manage finances, and may get lost in familiar places. Mood changes occur.

Moderate Demented

Middle-stage Alzheimer's. Major gaps in memory. Need assistance with daily living (dressing, cooking). Confusion about dates, locations, and personal history is frequent.
